What Is Behaviour Monitoring?

Monitoring Behaviour is a form of anomaly detection that analyzes and correlates user activity on a computer or network to identify events and patterns that may require further investigation.

Computers are used to monitor people’s behaviour in various environments.

  • Employers monitor productivity by tracking the websites workers are accessing.
  • Surveillance systems, such as CCTV cameras, are used to detect crime.
  • Ankle monitors are used to track people under house arrest.

How Can Behaviour Be Monitored And Who Monitors It?

Internet users' behaviour can be monitored in many different ways.

The primary form of behaviour monitoring is the use of cookies.
Cookies are messages web servers pass to your web browser when you visit internet sites.
When you request another page from your server, your browser sends the cookies back to the server.

The term 'cookie' is allusion to a Unix program called Fortune Cookie that produces a different message/fortune, each time it runs.
The most common form of cookie is used to track web activity.
The cookie itself acts as your identification card and every time you revisit the same site, your browser passes a cookie back to the server.
By doing this, the web server can gather information about which web pages the users visits the most and which pages are gathering the most repeat hits.

Security Concerns When It Comes To Using Cookies?

If you accept cookies for advertising companies, they can develop detailed profiles of the people who select their ads across their customers' sites.

Also, the websites that create cookies can read them and allows them to keep track of your interactions on that website.

In order to increase ad effectiveness (relevant user ads), ubiquitous tracking and ad-specificity are incorporated to achieve this.

However, a downside to this is that user privacy has to breached even further. A further blow to the user is that they don't collect the information with someone's consent.

You can change what information on what the cookies collect from you so you don't have to worry about the cookies being used to track your every movement. Instead they will just gather the general information on what you do unless you specify otherwise or refuse to consent to collect cookies.
